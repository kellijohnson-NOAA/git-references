
# Summary
Commercial fishers engage in a risky industry, where the chances of
experiencing death or major injury due to their occupation is significantly
higher than other occupations in the United States.
In the United States West Coast Limited Entry fixed-gear sablefish fishery,
fishers were less likely to embark in a fishing expedition if there were
high winds after the implementation of the catch share program than before
the program was initiated and fishers had to "race to fish."
