# Summary
A framework for the objective selection of a suite of indicators
to facilitate ecosystem-based management with respect to fisheries
management.

* avoid redundancy
* select as few as possible
* minimize the overall risk
* management actions may lead to new, unforeseen problems
* indicators give insight into the likely effects of actions
* linear variation in an indicator is not always true

# Framework

1. Users: Identify users who will define objectives, and then identify
the major threats to achieving the objectives, which will help in scoping
the indicators
2. Candidate list: It is important to include all users that could be of
interest to the indicators and then weed out indicators later
3. Screening: All 9 criteria should be used, but may have varying levels
of importance (e.g., "high", "med", "low"), each criteria will also be
of different interest to user groups (e.g., "scientists", "managers", "public")

  * Concreteness
  * Theoretical basis
  * Cost
  * Measurement
  * Historical data
  * Sensitivity
  * Responsiveness
  * Specificity

4. Scoring: Evaluate indicators against criteria, while simultaneously
assessing the quality of information used for the judgment;
bias, variance, accuracy, and precision will all play a roll
