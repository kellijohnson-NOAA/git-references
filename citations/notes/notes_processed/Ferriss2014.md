Summary
=======

Uses (Essington *et al.*, 2001)(notes/notes\_processed/Essington2001.md) methods to estimate consumption rates for tuna, while incorporating Hg-at-size, to test the hypothesis that key parameters governing growth and Hg dynamics may be estimable from patterns of Hg-at-size data.

Methods
=======

Bioenergetics models produce an estimated consumption rate-at-size, while contaminant models relate changes in Hg-at-size to consumption rate and growth.

Essington, T. E., Kitchell, J. F., and Walters, C. J. 2001. The von bertalanffy growth function, bioenergetics, and the consumption rates of fish. Canadian Journal of Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences, 58: 2129–2138. <http://www.nrcresearchpress.com/doi/abs/10.1139/f01-151> (Accessed 8 July 2014).
