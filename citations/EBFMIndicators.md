    Title: Quantitative ecosystem indicators for fisheries management

# Overview

A summary of articles presented in an ICES special issue [62(3)](http://icesjms.oxfordjournals.org/content/62/3.toc) on Quantitative Ecosystem Indicators for Fisheries Management. Articles are the result of a symposium focusing on using ecosystem indicators for fisheries management.

Indicators should be meaningful and accurately reflect the state of the ecosystem. Ecosystems involve both target and non-target species as well as the habitat in which these species live, and indicators should take all aspects into consideration. Therefore, management interventions will necessarily involve trade-offs.

# History of ecosystem-based indicators in fisheries management

-   1999: Government of Australia and FAO (Garcia and Staples, 2000) met to develop Technical Guidelines No. 8 for the FAO Code of Conduct for Responsible Fisheries: Indicators for sustainable development of marine capture fisheries.

-   2001 - 2004: SCOR/IOC Working Group 119 on Quantitative Ecosystem Indicators for Fisheries Management to develop the scientific basis of using indicators for an ecosystem approach to fisheries, review existing knowledge, evaluate the utility of new indicators, and develop frameworks for indicator implementation.

-   2005: ICES special issue on [indicators](http://icesjms.oxfordjournals.org/content/62/3.toc).

Garcia, S. M., and Staples, D. J. 2000. Sustainability reference systems and indicators for responsible marine capture fisheries: A review of concepts and elements for a set of guidelines. Marine and Freshwater Research, 51: 385–426. CSIRO. <http://www.publish.csiro.au/?paper=MF99092> (Accessed 4 May 2016).
